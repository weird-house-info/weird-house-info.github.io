Sorting Hat on a 19th-century weird house? Yes! ^_^

Adopted from National Register of Historic Places Inventory

The Pythian Castle is an excellent example of commercial Queen Anne architecture. Prominent among the century-old building's exterior features are the five towers: three round-corner ones with “witches hats" and two square bays in the centers of the street-side walls.


Sorting Hat character shares a lot of similarities with the tower roofs.

Curved brackets line the vertical-board cornice on the street sides: colonettes, pointed pediments, and brackets decorate the windows in the towers, and broken pediments top two pairs of windows in the south wall. The building has recently undergone major restoration/renovation work.


Exterior alterations are confined to ground-level changes in doors and windows.

Interior remodeling is extensive. However, intrusive exterior alterations have been corrected and interior work retained as many original features as possible. Located on a busy corner just two blocks north of Arcata's downtown Plaza, the Pythian Castle is the community's most notable 19th-century commercial building.


Baga’s castle and 11-th Street to the west.

The Pythian Castle is a two-story building of wood construction with 2,880 square feet on each floor. The steps and deck on the south side cover 640 square feet, and the north-side stairs from the upper floor total 148 square feet. Located on a corner two blocks north of Arcata's Plaza, the building is in an area of mixed architectural styles and periods. Newer buildings and parking areas occupy the other three corners of the intersection, but within the same block as the Pythian Castle are settlement-period houses, a Victorian, and two turn-of-the-century houses.


The Pythian Castle then and now.

The building's Queen Anne architecture is dominated by five projecting towers. Three round-corner towers contain five windows and are topped by "witches hats" that in times past sprouted tall finials. The two center-wall towers are square bays with four windows and truncated hip tops, once highlighted by cresting. Patterned shingles covered the tower roofs in the early days.

The bays are decorated with triangular pediments, colonettes, and brackets. A vertical-board cornice along the east and south walls is marked by a line of curved brackets. Two pairs of upper-story windows on the south wall are covered by broken pediments.
